01 THE BEGINNING OF TAKOPOPS

The journey of Takopops began during the post-pandemic era, a time when small businesses were thriving as people rallied to support local entrepreneurs. Many individuals, myself included, faced job loss and uncertainty, which pushed us to find new ways to make a living and pay our bills. Inspired by this shift, I decided to create my own business centered around a beloved dish—takoyaki—while tapping into the community's growing interest in supporting small, local ventures.

To start my journey, I borrowed a small retractable tent from my aunt and gathered basic cooking materials that I purchased online in small quantities. For two weeks, I set up shop at a local food bazaar, where I had the opportunity to introduce my unique takoyaki to the public. The response was incredible; I quickly built a loyal customer base. Many patrons reached out after the bazaar ended, eager to know if I would continue making takoyaki. This encouragement gave me the confidence to take a leap of faith and expand my operations.

With the support of my friends and family, I built a small stall right in front of our house, conveniently located near an elementary school. This strategic position allowed us to attract a steady stream of customers, who enjoyed our signature product—takoyaki on a stick. As the business flourished, so did my sense of responsibility. I used the earnings to help my family, purchasing items they had long desired but couldn’t afford, and contributing to home repairs. Through Takopops, I not only found a way to sustain my livelihood but also improved my family's living conditions, proving that with determination and support, we can rise above challenges.
